Key Trigonometric Identities

Fundamental formulas and relationships

Pythagorean Identity

sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1

The most fundamental trigonometric identity, derived from the Pythagorean theorem.

Reciprocal Identities

csc(θ) = 1/sin(θ)
sec(θ) = 1/cos(θ)
cot(θ) = 1/tan(θ)

Relationships between primary and reciprocal functions.

Quotient Identities

tan(θ) = sin(θ)/cos(θ)
cot(θ) = cos(θ)/sin(θ)

Express tangent and cotangent in terms of sine and cosine.

Even-Odd Identities

sin(-θ) = -sin(θ)
cos(-θ) = cos(θ)
tan(-θ) = -tan(θ)

Symmetry properties of trigonometric functions.

Co-function Identities

sin(90° - θ) = cos(θ)
cos(90° - θ) = sin(θ)
tan(90° - θ) = cot(θ)

Relationships between complementary angles.

Double Angle Formulas

sin(2θ) = 2sin(θ)cos(θ)
cos(2θ) = cos²(θ) - sin²(θ)
tan(2θ) = 2tan(θ)/(1 - tan²(θ))

Formulas for double angles.
